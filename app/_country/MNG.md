     desc: The task is a part of Arkhangai mapping project. In this project, a local NGO, Ger Community Mapping Center (GCMC), is collecting local data on impacts of climate change. GCMC aims to provide regional decision makers with fact-based climate change narratives by local communities. The average temperature in Mongolia has risen 2.1 degrees celsius since 1940 (3C by 2050). As a result, aridification &amp; deforestation have intensified across Mongolia and lives of people (herders mostly) in the countryside have been impacted as pasture and water resources deteriorate. Tsenkher soum is one of 19 soums of Arkhangai province, Mongolia. In Tsenkher soum, coupled with climate change impacts, gold mining activities have aggravated local vulnerability. Local community in Tsenkher soum is experiencing water pollution in rivers, soil erosion, households relocated, herders became miners, mining excavation on winter and spring camps of herders and land violations. The support from HOT OSM community will be instrumental in creating integrated geographic database of Arkhangai. Up-to-date, quality data means better decision making and better preparedness to climate change impacts.
